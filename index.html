The Solar System

The Solar System

The Solar System consists of the Sun and all the celestial objects gravitationally bound to it, including eight planets, dwarf planets, moons, asteroids, and comets. It formed about 4.5 billion years ago from a cloud of gas and dust, and is located in the Milky Way galaxy. The Sun, a star, is the central body, and the planets orbit it in elliptical paths.

Life Cycle Of Stars

Life Cycle Of Stars

Stars are born in nebulae, evolve through a main sequence phase, and eventually exhaust their fuel, leading to different fates depending on their mass. Low to medium mass stars become red giants, then planetary nebulae, and finally white dwarfs. Massive stars become red supergiants, explode as supernovae, and then collapse into either neutron stars or black holes.
